About Me


I’m a recent Bioengineering PhD graduate from Rice University currently working for a medical diagnostics start-up located at the NYU School of Dentistry in New York. My work focuses on developing a diagnostic device that leverages microfluidic design, scalable microfabrication processes, and statistical machine learning algorithms in order to identify early signs of oral cancer. The ultimate goal of this technology is to empower dentists to make difficult patient referral decisions with more confidence, shifting disease management further upstream where survival rates and prognoses are much more promising.
